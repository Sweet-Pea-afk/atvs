Foundations and Early Networking (1960s – 1970s)

Key Developments:

  1. Packet Switching Concepts - Innovated independently by Paul Baran (RAND Corporation) and Donald Davies (UK), packet switching enabled data to be broken into packets and routed dynamically—a radical departure from circuit-switched models
  2. ARPANET & Early Connectivity - ARPANET, funded by the U.S. Department of Defense, began taking shape with the first nodes linked by UCLA and SRI in 1969; the inaugural message ("LO") sent on October 29 sparked the birth of the modern Internet
  3. Protocols & Infrastructure - First RFCs issued in April 1969 Network Control Program (NCP), enabling Telnet and FTP, emerged around 1970
  4. Ethernet (1973, Xerox PARC), SATNET, first routers (~1974), and true IP routers (~1976) all marked critical forward leaps
  • UP NEXT: The Standardization and Expansion Era (1980s–1990s) – marked by the dominance of TCP/IP, the rise of the commercial Internet, and the widespread adoption of LAN technologies.
  • The Modern Era (2000s–Present) – a period characterized by Wi‑Fi evolution, virtualization, cloud computing, SDN, IoT, and 5G networks.
