Modern & Emerging Paradigms (2000s – Present)

Key Developments:

  1. Wi‑Fi Evolution & Security - Wi‑Fi evolved rapidly:
    • 802.11b/g in early 2000s (up to ~20 Mbps)
    • WPA/WPA2 security enhancements (2003–2004)
    • 802.11n in 2009 (higher speeds, dual-band)
    • WPA3 introduced in 2018 for improved security
  2. Ethernet Speeds Soar- Ethernet evolved from 3 Mbps to 100 Gbps, with progression through Fast Ethernet, Gigabit, and 10 Gigabit standards
  3. Cloud, Virtualization & SDN/NFV- Cloud computing and virtualization reshaped infrastructure, promoting scalability and flexibility
  4. Software‑Defined Networking (SDN) emerged in the mid‑2000s, decoupling control and data planes for programmability
  5. Network Function Virtualization (NFV), often paired with SDN, has become a core enabler—especially for building flexible 5G networks
  6. IoT, 5G & Looking Forward IoT's explosive growth introduced vast scale and variety to networking demands 30DC
  7. 5G networks promise ultra-low latency and massive connectivity for future applications
